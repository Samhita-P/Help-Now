First Aid Help at Your Fingertips

First Aid Guide

1. Bleeding

Minor Bleeding

  • Apply gentle pressure with a clean cloth.
  • Elevate the wound if possible.

Severe Bleeding

  • Apply firm pressure with a clean cloth or bandage.
  • Raise the injured area above heart level if possible.
  • Call emergency services immediately.

2. Burns

Minor Burns

  • Run cool water over the burn for 10 minutes.
  • Apply a clean dressing. Do not apply ointments or creams.

Severe Burns

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Do not remove clothing stuck to the burn.
  • Cover the area with a cool, moist cloth.

3. Choking

Adult Choking

  • Perform the Heimlich maneuver (abdominal thrusts) until the object is expelled.

Infant Choking

  • Give five back blows and five chest thrusts for infants under 1 year of age.

4. Heart Attack

  • Signs of Heart Attack: Chest pain, shortness of breath, nausea, sweating.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • If conscious, have the person chew an aspirin (if not allergic).

5. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

For Adults

  • If unresponsive and not breathing, give chest compressions at a depth of 2 inches.
  • Rate: 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • Follow with two rescue breaths.

For Children

  • Perform CPR with less pressure, about 1/3 the chest depth.

6. Severe Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)

  • Signs: Swelling of the face, lips, or throat, difficulty breathing, rash, hives.
  • Administer epinephrine if available.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person calm and monitor their breathing.

7. Stroke

  • Signs: Sudden numbness or weakness, difficulty speaking, confusion, or severe headache.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person as calm as possible and still.

8. Heatstroke

  • Signs: High body temperature, confusion, rapid pulse, hot, dry skin.
  • Move the person to a cool area immediately.
  • Give them cool water if conscious.
  • Apply cool, damp cloths to the body.
  • Call emergency services immediately.

9. Hypothermia

  • Signs: Shivering, confusion, slurred speech, and blue skin.
  • Move the person to a warm area immediately.
  • Remove wet clothes and warm them with blankets.
  • Offer warm drinks if they are conscious.
  • Call emergency services immediately.

10. Poisoning

  • Ingested Poison: Do not induce vomiting unless instructed by poison control.
  • Call Poison Control or emergency services immediately.
  • For chemical exposure, flush skin or eyes with water for at least 15 minutes.

11. Seizures

  • Do not try to restrain the person or hold them down.
  • Ensure the area is safe to prevent injury.
  • Place something soft under their head.
  • After the seizure, turn them on their side to help with breathing.
  • Call emergency services if the seizure lasts more than 5 minutes or is repeated.

12. Insect Stings/Bites

  • Remove the stinger with a scraping motion.
  • Wash the area with soap and water.
  • Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
  • If allergic reaction occurs, use epinephrine and call emergency services immediately.

13. Sprains and Strains

  • Rest the affected area and avoid weight-bearing activities.
  • Apply ice for 20-30 minutes to reduce swelling.
  • Elevate the injured area above heart level.
  • Compress with an elastic bandage if necessary.

14. Fractures (Broken Bones)

  • Immobilize the injured area using a splint or cloth.
  • Apply ice to reduce swelling.
  • Call emergency services for immediate medical attention.

15. Unconsciousness

  • Check for breathing and pulse.
  • If unresponsive and breathing, place the person in the recovery position.
  • If no breathing or pulse, begin CPR immediately and call emergency services.

16. Severe Head Injury

  • Check for signs of consciousness and breathing.
  • If unconscious, call emergency services immediately.
  • Do not move the person unless they are in danger (e.g., from traffic).
  • Apply a clean cloth to the head to stop bleeding.

17. Hyperventilation

  • Encourage the person to breathe slowly and deeply into a paper bag or cupped hands.
  • Calm them down by talking in a slow, reassuring manner.
  • If the person doesnâ€™t calm down, call emergency services.

18. Frostbite

  • Signs: Numbness, pale skin, cold feeling, and tingling sensations in the affected area.
  • Move the person to a warm area immediately.
  • Rewarm the affected area slowly with lukewarm water (not hot).
  • Avoid rubbing the frostbitten area.

19. Hypoglycemia (Low Blood Sugar)

  • Signs: Shaking, confusion, sweating, dizziness.
  • Give the person a source of sugar (e.g., juice, glucose tablets, candy).
  • If the person remains unconscious or unable to swallow, call emergency services immediately.

20. Heat Exhaustion

  • Signs: Heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, nausea, headache.
  • Move to a cooler environment and hydrate the person with cool water.
  • Apply cool, damp cloths to their skin to bring down their body temperature.
  • If symptoms persist, call emergency services.

21. Anaphylaxis (Severe Allergic Reaction)

  • Signs: Swelling of the face, difficulty breathing, hives, dizziness.
  • Administer an epinephrine injection if available.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person in a comfortable position and monitor breathing.

22. Dehydration

  • Signs: Dry mouth, dizziness, excessive thirst, dark-colored urine.
  • Encourage the person to drink water or electrolyte solutions slowly.
  • Move them to a cooler environment if heat is the cause.
  • If symptoms worsen, call emergency services.

23. Asthma Attack

  • Signs: Shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing, chest tightness.
  • Help the person use their prescribed inhaler.
  • If the symptoms persist for more than 10 minutes or worsen, seek emergency help.

24. Panic Attack

  • Signs: Rapid breathing, chest pain, sweating, dizziness.
  • Help the person focus on slow, deep breathing.
  • Encourage them to calm down and assure them the symptoms will pass.
  • If the person feels like theyâ€™re going to faint or if symptoms worsen, call emergency services.

25. Choking on Food or Object

  • If the person cannot breathe or speak, perform the Heimlich maneuver.
  • If they lose consciousness, begin CPR immediately.
  • If they are conscious and coughing, encourage them to cough forcefully.

26. Snake Bites

  • Stay calm and try to identify the snake if possible.
  • Keep the affected area still and below the heart level.
  • Call emergency services immediately for antivenom treatment.
  • Do not attempt to suck out the venom.

27. Animal Scratches

  • Wash the scratched area thoroughly with soap and water.
  • Apply an antiseptic and cover with a sterile bandage.
  • If the animal was potentially rabid, seek medical attention immediately.

28. Toxic Gas Exposure

  • Evacuate the area and move the person to fresh air immediately.
  • If the person is unconscious, call emergency services right away.
  • Seek medical treatment if symptoms of poisoning or suffocation appear.

29. Severe Abdominal Pain

  • Monitor for signs of internal bleeding (e.g., fainting, dizziness).
  • Do not give food or drink, as this could worsen the situation.
  • Call emergency services to rule out conditions like appendicitis or a ruptured organ.

30. Poison Ivy Exposure

  • Wash the affected area with soap and water as soon as possible.
  • Apply an over-the-counter ointment like hydrocortisone or calamine lotion.
  • If the rash is severe, seek medical attention.

31. Dizziness

  • Help the person lie down in a comfortable position.
  • Ensure they are hydrated and cool.
  • If dizziness persists or is associated with other symptoms, call emergency services.

32. High Blood Pressure Crisis

  • Signs: Severe headache, chest pain, blurred vision, shortness of breath.
  • Keep the person calm and seated in a relaxed position.
